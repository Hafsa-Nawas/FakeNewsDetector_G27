Overview

What is this research about?

The rapid spread of fake news on social media platforms undermines public trust and decision. Traditional ML models achieve low accuracy due to limited contextual analysis.

This study proposes a Hybrid BiGRU-Transformer model that processes news text, image-extracted text (via EasyOCR), and social media reactions (likes, shares, sentiment ratios) for fake news classification.

The model fuses these modalities ‚Äî TF-IDF vectorization for text, VADER sentiment analysis, and numerical reaction features ‚Äî into a powerful detection system that surpasses traditional models by leveraging deep contextual understanding.

Methodology

How the Model Works

Three-phase experimental design: data collection, model development, and system evaluation.

Model Architecture

BiGRU captures bidirectional sequential context from combined text. Transformer attention handles long-range dependencies. Numeric MLP processes social reaction features. All fused before final classification.

01

Data Collection

Collected from WELFake, FakeNewsNet (BuzzFeed + PolitiFact), and custom social media reactions dataset. 44,000+ rows combined.

02

Preprocessing

TF-IDF vectorization (512 features), VADER sentiment scoring, log-scaling of reaction counts, and label encoding.

03

Model Training

Stratified K-Fold (k=5) cross-validation. Adam optimizer, class-weighted loss function to handle class imbalance.
