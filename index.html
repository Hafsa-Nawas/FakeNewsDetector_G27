What is this research about?

The rapid spread of misinformation on social media platforms poses significant challenges to public trust and decision-making. Most existing approaches rely solely on text-based analysis and fail to capture the full picture of how fake news spreads online.

This research focuses on integrating three key features into a unified detection framework â€” news article text, image-extracted text via OCR, and social media reaction signals â€” to build a more comprehensive understanding of misinformation.

Core Goal: This work explores how integrating text content, visual text from images, and user engagement patterns can work together to study fake news behaviour on social media platforms.

Data Collection

WELFake (44K+ news articles), FakeNewsNet (BuzzFeed + PolitiFact), and custom-collected social media reactions from Facebook & Instagram.

02

Preprocessing

TF-IDF vectorization (512 features), VADER sentiment scoring, EasyOCR image text extraction, log-scaling of reaction counts, and label encoding.

03

Model Training

Stratified K-Fold (k=5) cross-validation. Adam optimizer with class-weighted loss to handle class imbalance across the fused feature set.
